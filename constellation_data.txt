[Orion]
Alias: The Hunter
Major Stars: Betelgeuse, Rigel, Bellatrix, Mintaka, Alnilam, Saiph
Fact: Orion is one of the most recognizable constellations and represents a hunter from Greek mythology.

[Ursa Major]
Alias: The Great Bear
Major Stars: Dubhe, Merak, Phecda, Megrez, Alioth, Mizar, Alkaid
Fact: Ursa Major contains the Big Dipper asterism, which is often used to find the North Star.

[Ursa Minor]
Alias: The Little Bear
Major Stars: Polaris, Kochab, Pherkad, Epsilon UMi, Zeta UMi, Eta UMi, Yildun
Fact: Ursa Minor includes Polaris, the current North Star.

[Cassiopeia]
Alias: The Queen
Major Stars: Schedar, Caph, Gamma Cas, Ruchbah, Segin
Fact: Cassiopeia is shaped like a W and is prominent in the northern sky.

[Cygnus]
Alias: The Swan
Major Stars: Deneb, Sadr, Albireo, Gienah
Fact: Cygnus is known as the Northern Cross and lies on the plane of the Milky Way.

[Taurus]
Alias: The Bull
Major Stars: Aldebaran, Elnath, Pleione
Fact: Taurus contains the bright star Aldebaran and the Pleiades star cluster.

[Gemini]
Alias: The Twins
Major Stars: Castor, Pollux, Alhena, Wasat
Fact: Gemini represents the twins Castor and Pollux from Greek mythology.

[Leo]
Alias: The Lion
Major Stars: Regulus, Algieba, Zosma, Chort, Denebola, Adhafera
Fact: Leo is a zodiac constellation best seen in spring.

[Scorpius]
Alias: The Scorpion
Major Stars: Antares, Shaula, Sargas, Dschubba
Fact: Scorpius is one of the most prominent constellations of the southern sky.

[Lyra]
Alias: The Lyre
Major Stars: Vega, Sheliak, Sulafat
Fact: Lyra contains Vega, the fifth-brightest star in the night sky.
